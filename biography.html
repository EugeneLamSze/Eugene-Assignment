Biographies of Famous Scientists

James Watson

Watson was born in Chicago, Illinois on April 6, 1928. He earned his BSc at the University of Chicago, his PhD at Indiana University and his post-doctoral year at the University of Copenhagen. He worked at the University of Cambridge where he and Francis Crick discovered the double helical structure of DNA. Watson was awarded as Noble laureate when he was a professor at Harvard University in 1962.

Frederick Banting

Banting was born on November 14, 1891, in Essa, Ontario. Banting graduated with his Bachelor of Medicine (M.B.) at the University of Toronto in 1916. He served in the military during WWI after his graduation. He returned to medical practice and teaching at the University of Western Ontario and University of Toronto after the war and received his MD in 1922. With the success of isolating insulin from pancreas in cattle in 1921. Banting administered the first ever insulin injection in diabetic patients in 1922.

Charles Kao Kuen

Kao was born in Shanghai on November 4, 1933 . He obtained his Bachelor degree in electrical engineering in 1957 and his PhD in 1965 at the University of London. In the 1960s, Kao performed his pioneering work in creating ultra high purity fibre optics as a telecommunications medium. He was awarded the Nobel Prize for Physics in 2009 for his ‘groundbreaking achievements concerning the transmission of light in fibres for optical communication’.
